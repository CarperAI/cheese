Sunlight reaches Earth's atmosphere and is scattered in all directions by all the gases and particles in the air. Blue light is scattered more than the other colors because it travels as shorter, smaller waves. This is why we see a blue sky most of the time.

A goose is a bird in the family Anatidae, which also includes ducks and swans. Many people classify geese within the genera Anser and Branta as “true geese,” to distinguish them from other birds in the same family, some of whom confusingly have common names which include the term “goose” although they are not, in fact, geese.
A goose is a bird of any of several waterfowl species in the family Anatidae. This group comprises the genera Anser (the grey geese and white geese) and Branta (the black geese). Some other birds, mostly related to the shelducks, have "goose" as part of their names. More distantly related members of the family Anatidae are swans, most of which are larger than true geese, and ducks, which are smaller.
